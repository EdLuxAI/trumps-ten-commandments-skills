## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `carrot-and-stick-prisoners-dilemma`
The core operator. Every defensive operator in this skill is a response to or corollary of this
pattern. The prisoner's-dilemma academic framing makes the pattern legible and the counter-move
(pre-commit collectively) derivable.

### 2. `collective-action-as-only-counter`
The load-bearing defender operator. The Ben Franklin framing is memorable and the book case
(2020 BLNU statement — 94 CEOs in 12 hours) makes the "convene early" counter-move concrete.

### 3. `target-the-weakest-member-first`
Predictive operator — lets a coalition see the next target before the attack lands and
pre-position defense. Decision effect: who gets surrounded publicly first.

### 4. `wedge-issue-selection`
The operator that makes collective action fail even when the coalition is willing. The counter
("refuse selective exemptions publicly") requires members to absorb real costs for coalition
benefit, which is why this is the hardest to apply in practice.

### 5. `alliance-attacks`
Meta-operator about the infrastructure of collective response. The counter-move (fund independent
convening capacity) is longer-term than the others but has higher long-term decision delta.

### 6. `enlarge-the-pie-as-constructive-divide-and-conquer`
Included despite being a how-to rather than a defensive operator because the Abraham Accords
material is genuinely illuminating about how the same underlying pattern can be used
constructively. The `do_not_use_when` boundary (you need actual resources to back the positive
vision) is load-bearing — vague vision-casting discredits the approach.
