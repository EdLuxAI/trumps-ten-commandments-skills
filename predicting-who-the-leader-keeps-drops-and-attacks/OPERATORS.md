## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `winner-vs-loser-by-constituency-not-virtue`
The master sort rule. Every other operator in this skill is a corollary. Constituency is the
master variable. Decision effect: users learn to monitor their constituency, not their
contributions.

### 2. `rehabilitate-if-constituency-remains`
The rehabilitation logic. Counterintuitive for observers used to loyalty-based retention:
severity of prior criticism is not disqualifying; it is trophy value. Decision effect for users
who are former critics: prior criticism may be leverage, not liability.

### 3. `drop-invented-allies-keep-sovereign-ones`
The disposability logic. The Musk/Prigozhin parallel is the memorable case — too powerful to
discard, too independent to embrace. Decision effect: the operator predicts who is safe and who
isn't.

### 4. `drown-the-squeaky-wheel-in-oil`
The absorption default. Most recurrent operator in the source (appears across Ch. 5, Ch. 7, and
the intro's Trump-Sonnenfeld first meeting story). Often the first move, before attack. Decision
effect: users being offered access must consciously choose whether to accept.

### 5. `selective-retribution`
Refutes the common misperception that dominance-seeking leaders are indiscriminately vindictive.
The selection rule (unsympathetic + isolated) is specific and predictive. Decision effect:
users who might become targets can work on the "unsympathetic" and "isolated" conditions before
the attack lands.

### 6. `push-retribution-until-system-pushes-back`
Forward-looking operator. Predicts the probing pattern and suggests that institutional defenders
can succeed when they maintain the boundaries. Decision effect: supports institutional strategy
over tactical firefighting.

### 7. `strike-adjacent-weakness`
The chain-attack operator. Predicts that primary targets are rarely hit directly; adjacencies
are. Decision effect: users adjacent to primary targets know they may be the attack vector.

### 8. `preemptive-retaliation-via-asymmetric-leverage`
The Roffman case. Specific mechanism for silencing junior critics via institutional leverage.
Decision effect: suggests "build direct audience reach" as a structural defense.
