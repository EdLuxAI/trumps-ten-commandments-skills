# Rejected Candidates

## Folded into existing operators

### `refer-to-self-in-third-person` / "Sir" addressing
Ch. 10 material on Trump's third-person self-reference and addressing himself as "Sir" in
retold stories. Vivid illustration of operator 2 (`grandeur-as-armor`) but not a distinct
operator. Kept as case material.

### `John-Barron-pseudonym`
Trump's 1980s-1990s practice of impersonating a fictitious spokesman ("John Barron" — slight
voice alteration, calls to reporters praising Donald Trump in the third person). Exemplifies
operator 2 at its most literal. Case material.

### `inaugural-crowd-size-inflation`
Classic case of operator 2 (inflated numbers even when unflated would be impressive). Specific
illustration, not new operator.

### `decoy-elevator-buttons`
Trump-tower elevators have more floor buttons than floors; extra buttons are non-functional.
Illustration of operator 2's structural commitment to inflated appearance. Great case material,
not distinct operator.
