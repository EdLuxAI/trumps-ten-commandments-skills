## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `messianic-framing-I-alone-can-fix-it`
The rhetorical signature of grandiosity. Pairs with skill #1's `hub-and-spokes-detection` — the
rhetoric and the org structure are correlated. Including both makes the pattern visible at
message level and at structural level.

### 2. `grandeur-as-armor-for-insecurity`
The psychological claim. Most decision-shaping implication: the insecurity determines which
criticisms land. Insider-diagnostic (Susie Wiles via Whipple) is unusually credible —
demonstrates the pattern is visible to chief-of-staff-level insiders.

### 3. `class-for-the-masses`
Why the aesthetic that looks repellent to elite observers is precisely the point. Veblen's 1899
prediction is the academic backing. Counter-move is specifically to *not* critique the
aesthetic — a counterintuitive rule for many users.

### 4. `physical-monuments-as-legacy`
Operational operator. Predicts where the leader's attention and capital will go. Shelley's
*Ozymandias* is the book's own coda, referenced here.

### 5. `fragility-under-mockery`
The most actionable operator in this skill. The specific counterintuitive rule — light mockery
wounds, angry confrontation fortifies — is the single piece of advice from this skill that
most users should act on. Labeled `boundary` because it specifies which criticisms penetrate
and which don't.

### 6. `prefer-bilateral-to-multilateral`
Predictive operator for diplomatic and commercial format choices. Dual-use: defender's warning
(preserve multilateral infrastructure) and bilateral counterparty's opportunity.

### 7. `heroic-identity-vs-heroic-mission`
Sonnenfeld's own distinction from *Hero's Farewell* (1988). Used here as the master diagnostic —
if the leader is heroic-mission-dominant, this skill doesn't apply; if heroic-identity-dominant,
every other operator fires.

### 8. `article-II-unbounded-authority`
The forward-looking operator. Predicts institutional override. Schlesinger's *Imperial
Presidency* (1973) as the academic foundation makes clear this is a decades-long American
political-science tradition, not a novel 2025 observation.
