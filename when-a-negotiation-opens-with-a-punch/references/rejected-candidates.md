# Rejected Candidates

## Cut because duplicates another operator in this skill

### `defer-decision-as-power`
From Ch. 1: the leader refuses to close options to preserve maximum leverage. Overlaps with
`TACO-pattern-recognition` (the other side's apparent indecision may be strategic, not weak) and
with `artificial-urgency-deadline` (manufactured urgency is the inverse move). Decision: folded as
a sub-observation under operator 6 rather than a standalone. The core counter-advice — don't read
delay as weakness — is captured.

## Cut because belongs in another skill

### `lawfare-as-attrition`
Ch. 2 has extensive material on Trump's 4,000+ lawsuits as weapons of attrition. As a negotiation
tactic — "the lawsuit is itself the pressure, irrespective of merit" — it appears in this skill
as operator 5 (disproportionate escalation). As a narrative-warfare tool (PR amplification via
lawsuits against media, book authors, political opponents) it belongs in skill #5
(`fighting-a-lie-that-gets-repeated-into-truth`). Splitting the material avoids duplication.

### `projection-claim-aggrieved-victim`
Ch. 2 passage "Projecting His Own Attributes Preemptively onto Others" describes accusing the
counterparty of exactly the tactic you are using (fake news, lawfare, witch hunts). This is
primarily a narrative-warfare operator, not a negotiation operator. Moved to skill #5.

### `lawsuit-as-PR-amplifier`
The technique of filing obviously meritless suits to generate coverage. Belongs in skill #5.

## Cut as color

### Real estate biographical detail
The Commodore Hotel, Grand Hyatt, Atlantic City bankruptcies, Trump Tower construction — rich
material for understanding how Trump's negotiation style formed, but not decision-shaping for a
user facing a shock-opener today. Parked in `cases.md` only where a case illustrates an active
operator (e.g., Mar-a-Lago for `leverage-by-adjacent-assets`).

### The Neil Barsky / Wall Street Journal entrapment
A Ch. 3 story: Trump offered a reporter tickets through a subordinate, then publicly attacked the
reporter for accepting — forcing the *Journal* to pull the reporter off the beat. Belongs in skill
#5 (narrative warfare) as a specific tactic of setting-up-then-attacking-journalists. Not a
negotiation operator.

## Cut as political framing

### Second-term monetization cases (crypto, Qatar 747, Executive Branch Club)
Politically loaded and rapidly dating. The underlying pattern — mixing personal interest with
official role — is captured in skill #1 as cash-flow detection signals for
`hub-and-spokes-detection`. Leaving the specifics out of this negotiation skill keeps it
usable across political contexts.

## Cut as generic

### "Trust, but verify" (Reagan on Soviet arms negotiations)
Generic negotiation wisdom. Does not add decision delta beyond what operator 8
(`post-deal-implementation-neglect`) already provides.
