## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `perpetual-noise-machine`
The organizing pattern. Spector's Wall of Sound (music, 1960s) gives the skill its memorable
frame. Counter-move cross-references skill #5 for sustained message repetition.

### 2. `release-transgressive-material-voluntarily`
The most counterintuitive operator. Ukraine transcript release is the canonical case. Folded
`self-foiling-backfire` as a sub-signal because it's the same mechanism that occasionally fails.

### 3. `rage-bait-harness-reaction`
Defender-heavy. Recognition is usually easy; refusing the expected reaction is the hard part.

### 4. `manageable-chaos-vs-real-chaos`
A distinction operator rather than a detect-and-counter pattern. Telling which mode the opponent
is in changes the counter-move. Keep as core operator because the signals are genuinely distinct.

### 5. `inexplicable-diversion`
The covfefe case makes this operator memorable. Decision effect: refuse to decode.

### 6. `need-for-rotating-foils`
Bridges Ch. 5 and Ch. 6 material. Connects with skill #7's insult-as-weapon (where the specific
nickname mechanics live) and with skill #5's projection pattern.

### 7. `relentless-focus-on-your-own-message`
The meta-rule. Load-bearing for sustained organizational response. The specific sub-moves
(pre-commit to 2-3 messages, accept missing some provocations, defend stamina) are what convert
general advice to action.

  coalition coordination.

  Skill #7 covers the nickname mechanics; this skill covers the rotation cadence.
