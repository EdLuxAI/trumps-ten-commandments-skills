# Source Notes

## Primary source

**Sonnenfeld & Tian, *Trump's Ten Commandments* (2025), Chapter 6** ("The Wall of Sound"), with
material from Ch. 5 (rotating foils) and the Conclusion (focus discipline).

## Source caveats

`is_primary_source: false`. Many of the specific cases in Ch. 6 are politically charged
(Cohen/Kim Jong Un summit timing, Zelenskyy call transcript, Epstein cascade, Access Hollywood /
Clinton-accusers press event). The operators describe a general information-warfare pattern
independent of who is deploying it.

A specific caveat: Sonnenfeld frames the Ukraine transcript release and the Zelenskyy call
itself as unambiguous extortion. This is contested (Trump's impeachment proceeded on this basis;
the Senate did not convict). The operator (`release-transgressive-material-voluntarily`) does
not depend on the extortion framing being adjudicated in either direction — what matters for the
operator is the voluntary-release-during-bad-news-cycle mechanism, which is visible regardless
of how the underlying conduct is judged.

## Academic and historical lineage per operator

### `perpetual-noise-machine`

- **Phil Spector** (1960s), music producer. "Wall of Sound" — layered instruments, doubled parts,
  reverb, creating a dense, overwhelming sonic mass. Spector's technique is the metaphor
  Sonnenfeld borrows for the chapter's title and framing.
- **Herbert Simon**, *Sciences of the Artificial* (1969), "attention economy" concept: "A wealth
  of information creates a poverty of attention."
- **Thomas Davenport and John Beck**, *The Attention Economy: Understanding the New Currency of
  Business* (Harvard Business School Press, 2001). Extended Simon for the information-economy
  era.
- **Steve Bannon**'s "flood the zone with shit" (reported by Michael Lewis, 2018) — the crudest
  practitioner-side formulation. Not cited in the book but the relevant frame.

### `release-transgressive-material-voluntarily`

- No formal academic source. The counterintuitive logic is Sonnenfeld's analytical claim: the
  move is a diversion play, not a blunder.

### `rage-bait-harness-reaction`

- No formal academic lineage. Related concept: **emotional contagion** (Hatfield, Cacioppo,
  Rapson, 1994) in social psychology — expressed emotion spreads through groups. The opponent is
  weaponizing this by engineering the triggering stimulus.

### `manageable-chaos-vs-real-chaos`

- No formal academic lineage. The distinction is Sonnenfeld's ("wildfire vs. controlled burn").
  Useful precisely because it gives the user a diagnostic rather than a one-size-fits-all
  response.

### `inexplicable-diversion`

- No formal academic lineage. The covfefe case (May 2017) is the book's definitive illustration.
- Related concept: **curiosity gap** in attention research — incomplete or inexplicable
  information generates sustained attempts at closure.

### `need-for-rotating-foils`

- Related concept: **out-group homogeneity** and **scapegoating** in social psychology (Tajfel,
  1970s; Allport's *Nature of Prejudice*, 1954). The rhetorical need for a foil serves the same
  psychological function these traditions describe.

### `relentless-focus-on-your-own-message`

- **Davenport & Beck** on attention economy provides the economic framing: attention is a scarce
  resource that must be allocated. A defender who spreads attention across every provocation is
  being outbid by an attacker who concentrates.
