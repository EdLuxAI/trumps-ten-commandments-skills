## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `heads-I-win-tails-you-lose structure detection`
Master operator. Every other operator in this skill is a sub-pattern or failure mode of this
master. Decision-shaping for due diligence.

### 2. `buy-distressed-from-desperate-sellers`
Dual-side operator — the counter-move flips depending on whether you are the buyer being offered
the deal or the seller being approached. Distinct decision paths.

### 3. `other-peoples-money-default`
The most recognizable Trump pattern. Easy to detect; the counter (demand symmetric exposure) is
often negotiable but requires the user to know to ask.

### 4. `internecine-creditor-warfare`
Mirrors skill #4's `carrot-and-stick-prisoners-dilemma` but applied to financial counterparties
rather than political ones. Same underlying dynamic, different domain. Cross-referenced.

### 5. `overleverage-as-failure-mode`
Dual-audience operator: detect in others (they're more vulnerable than they appear) and detect in
yourself (you're becoming the bag-holder you thought you were avoiding). The Atlantic City
bankruptcies are the canonical case.

### 6. `brand-licensing-pure-upside`
Included because the late-career Trump business model is almost entirely this structure. Decision
effect: the user considering a brand-licensed venture should treat brand uplift as zero in their
base-case model and price the royalty as pure cost.
