## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `relentless-repetition-beats-accuracy`
The master operator. Hovland-Weiss sleeper effect (1951) is the foundational research. Two
sub-signals folded in: `confident-false-beats-hedged-true` and `single-message-for-mass-audience`.
Folding rather than listing separately because the counter-move (match frequency, match
confidence, one-sentence correction) is unified.

### 2. `define-the-counterparty-before-they-do`
Paired with `nickname-branding` in skill #7 (`handling-strategic-insults-and-mockery`). Here the
focus is **defender's preemption** — you define yourself before the attacker defines you. There
the focus is on **identifying and responding to** a branding attack.

### 3. `control-flow-of-records`
The evidentiary asymmetry that makes sleeper-effect campaigns viable. Counter-move (proactive
disclosure) has high decision delta.

### 4. `echo-a-useful-external-narrative`
Recognizes that not all false claims originate with the attacker. The laundering move
(amplification + plausible deniability) requires a specific counter.

### 5. `asymmetric-evidence-acceptance`
Labeled `anti_pattern` — the user's job is to recognize and document the pattern, not to mirror
it. Counter-move (tie own credibility to visible reversals) imposes a real cost on the defender
but is load-bearing.

### 6. `plant-seeds-and-water-them`
The timescale operator. Sleeper-effect campaigns unfold over years. This operator makes the long
horizon visible. The Mt. Rushmore and Nobel Peace Prize examples make the pattern memorable.

### 7. `projection-claim-aggrieved-victim`
Cross-references skill #2 (`when-a-negotiation-opens-with-a-punch`) where projection appears as a
negotiation tactic. Here the focus is on its narrative-warfare function.

### 8. `challenge-false-claims-with-equal-vigor`
The meta defender rule from the Conclusion. Its most valuable specific form: celebrate your own
wins when true; don't surrender narrative by silence.
