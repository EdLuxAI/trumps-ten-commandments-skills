## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `hub-and-spokes-detection`
The organizing diagnostic. Most recurrent pattern in the book ‚Äî appears in Ch. 1, Ch. 3, Ch. 7.
Detection signals expanded to include Ch. 4 ethics-diagnostic material (mix-personal-and-official
interest, related-party LLC networks) because those cash-flow patterns are observable manifestations
of the same centralization. Decision effect: determines whether the rest of the skill applies at all.

### 2. `acting-vs-confirmed-leverage`
Narrow but sharp. Single-chunk source but high decision delta for anyone considering a role under a
centralizer. Unique to this skill ‚Äî generalizes poorly outside centralized orgs.

### 3. `plausible-deniability-via-vague-suggestion`
Labeled as `anti_pattern` because the subordinate-side reading is "recognize the trap, don't
execute." The "meddlesome priest" framing (Henry II, 1170) is the book's own reference and makes the
operator memorable. Counter-move (never execute on inferred intent without written direction) has
very high decision delta.

### 4. `elimination-tournament-selection`
Recurs across Ch. 1 (cabinet picks, VP selection), Ch. 7 (fresh version in second-term appointments).
The counter-move ‚Äî "opting out of the tournament preserves independent stature" ‚Äî is
counterintuitive and specifically valued.

### 5. `reach-before-public-commitment`
The single most practical operator for anyone trying to influence a centralizer. Not a detection
operator ‚Äî a timing rule. Load-bearing for downstream skills #2, #4, #5.

### 6. `loyalty-is-one-way-and-vengeance-is-selective`
Paired with `winner-vs-loser-by-constituency` in skill #8 (`reading-winner-sort-and-retribution`).
Here the focus is the exit decision for an insider; there the focus is predicting who gets targeted
in the wider population. Cross-referenced in both skills.

### 7. `invert-hierarchy`
Drawn primarily from Ch. 3 (military) but the pattern also runs through Ch. 1's staffing logic.
Included here rather than in skill #4 (divide-and-conquer) because the primary decision effect is
about how to protect yourself as a displaced senior professional, which is a working-under-the-leader
question.

### 8. `no-true-friends-only-followers`
A boundary operator. Single-sentence rule ("do not mistake warmth for friendship") with large
downstream decision effect. Needs the private-charm context from Ch. 5 to land without seeming
cartoonish ‚Äî hence the emphasis in the counter-move that private warmth is *real in the moment*.
