## Cut as color

### The "Mexican rapists to salsa dancing" pivot
2016 anti-immigrant rhetoric softened to Spanish-language ads and salsa dancing in 2024 swing
states. Illustrates operator 6 (`unpredictability-as-draw`) and the Sonnenfeld observation that
"even insults can shift on a dime" when utility demands. Kept in cases.md.

### Rally music playlist curation
Sonnenfeld's point that Trump personally curates rally playlists — "YMCA," "You Can't Always Get
What You Want," Andrea Bocelli at the White House. The subversiveness of unexpected music is
thematically related to operator 6 but is not itself a decision-shaping operator. Color only.

### Ted Turner / Howard Stern genealogy
Background for why insult-as-entertainment works. Relevant as source-notes lineage but not a
distinct operator.

### "I don't like to analyze myself because I might not like what I see"
Ch. 10 material (more properly belongs in skill #8 on grandeur). Tells us about the insulter's
self-regard but doesn't generate a counter-move here.

### "Stick and stones may break my bones..."

### "Don't feed the troll"

more specificity — not-feeding isn't always silence; it's often a venue or format shift.
