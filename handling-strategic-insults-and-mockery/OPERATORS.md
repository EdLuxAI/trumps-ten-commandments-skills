## Per-Operator Notes

### 1. `nickname-branding`
The most recognizable insult operator. Partners with skill #5's
`define-the-counterparty-before-they-do` — this skill handles the detection+response side, skill
#5 handles the preemption side.

### 2. `disproportionate-counterpunch`
Cross-referenced with skill #2's `disproportionate-escalation-when-challenged` (Cohn playbook).
There the focus is on negotiation-context escalation; here on personal/public-insult context.

### 3. `humor-as-permission-granter`
The most important operator for detection. The mechanism (audience laugh = permission) is
specific enough to act on. The counter (deny the laugh by changing venue or withholding the
straight-man role) has high decision delta.

### 4. `own-the-insult-never-apologize`
Important because it explains why the obvious counter (demand apology) fails. The
"force consequences instead" alternative is load-bearing.

### 5. `insult-as-attention-currency`
The economic framing. Connects to skill #6 (flood-the-zone) — attention is scarce, insult is
currency, the insulter is rational within their market. Counter-move (create your own
attention events) is operational, not philosophical.

### 6. `unpredictability-as-draw`
Moved from skill #6 where it didn't quite fit. Here it explains why strategic insulters vary
their attacks — and why the defender's counter is consistency, not matching variety.

### 7. `pick-your-own-battlefield`
The Conclusion's defender rule. Load-bearing for strategic choice of venue/medium/tempo.
